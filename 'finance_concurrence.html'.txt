    <section>
        <h2>Specific Areas Requiring Finance Concurrence</h2>
        <h3>1. Manual Deviations</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Approving deviations from established procedures (Clause 1.1.4).</li>
            <li>Approving new processes not defined in the manual (Clause 1.1.5).</li>
        </ul>
        
        <h3>2. Procurement and Estimation</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Procurement through Project Management Consultant (PMC) (Clause 1.1.13).</li>
            <li>Using evaluation methods other than the lowest item-wise basis (Clause 1.3.2).</li>
            <li>Approving estimates above Rs. 25 lakh (Clause 2.1.1 (vi)).</li>
            <li>Estimates for Inventory Control (IC) items (Clause 2.1.2 (ii)).</li>
        </ul>

        <h3>3. Purchase Requisitions (PR)</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>PRs for IC items above Rs. 25 lakh (Clause 2.2.4.2 and 2.2.4.3).</li>
            <li>PRs for proprietary items above Rs. 25 lakh (Clause 2.2.5.4).</li>
            <li>PRs for Non-Inventory Control (NIC) items exceeding the Departmental Operating Procedure (DOP) value limits (Clause 2.2.6).</li>
        </ul>

        <h3>4. Tender Documents</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Standard commercial tender documents and any changes to tender documents (Clause 3.1.2).</li>
        </ul>

        <h3>5. Special Approvals</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Technical pre-qualification criteria and similar item definitions, though not required in the Refineries Division (Clause 3.3.3.1 and 3.3.3.5).</li>
            <li>Tendering without budget approval for capital procurement (Clause 3.10.10).</li>
            <li>Nomination of committee members for purchase (Clause 3.17.7).</li>
            <li>In-principle approval for Techno-commercial MoUs (Clause 3.26).</li>
        </ul>
    </section>

    <section>
        <h2>Role of Finance in Purchase Requisition (PR) Process</h2>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Estimate Preparation</strong>: Indenting department prepares an estimate.</li>
            <li><strong>Finance Scrutiny</strong>: Finance department checks for budget provisions, justification, correct rate adoption, compliance with DOP clauses.</li>
            <li><strong>Financial Concurrence</strong>: Necessary for administrative approval.</li>
            <li><strong>Post-Tender Scrutiny</strong>: Ensuring compliance with procedural instructions and justification for not selecting the lowest bid.</li>
            <li><strong>Final Concurrence and Entry</strong>: Ensuring no conflict of interest in future financial audits or payments.</li>
        </ul>
    </section>

    <section>
        <h2>Finance Concurrence in Tendering Process</h2>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Scrutiny and Verification</strong>: Checking compliance with procedural instructions, financial regulations, and administrative approvals.</li>
            <li><strong>Evaluation and Approval</strong>: Ensuring proposals are for the lowest acceptable tender and all costs are accurately calculated.</li>
            <li><strong>Conflict of Interest Management</strong>: Avoiding involvement in future financial audits or payments post-concurrence.</li>
        </ul>
    </section>

    <section>
        <h2>Finance Role in Commercial Bid Analysis (CBA)</h2>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Scrutiny of Tender Papers</strong>: Ensuring procedural compliance and thorough preparation.</li>
            <li><strong>Verification</strong>: Confirming administrative and procedural approvals.</li>
            <li><strong>Evaluation</strong>: Ensuring lowest acceptable tender and clear terms and conditions.</li>
            <li><strong>Negotiation Records</strong>: Proper maintenance of negotiation records.</li>
            <li><strong>Compliance and Accuracy</strong>: Ensuring adherence to government procurement policies and accuracy of financial data.</li>
        </ul>
    </section>

    <section>
        <h2>Summary</h2>
        <p>The finance department's role is crucial in maintaining financial propriety, ensuring compliance with guidelines, and avoiding conflicts of interest throughout the procurement and tendering processes. This ensures transparency, financial discipline, and alignment with the organization's financial policies.</p>
